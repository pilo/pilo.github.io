# My Journey

## From Restaurant Owner to F&B Procurement Specialist

### The Beginning

My journey in the food & beverage industry started on the operational side. I owned and operated a restaurant, where I learned firsthand what it means to run a hospitality business — from managing daily operations to handling suppliers, controlling costs, and ensuring customer satisfaction.

### Selling the Restaurant

After years of dedicated work, I made the decision to sell the restaurant. This was a significant milestone — not just professionally, but personally as well. It gave me the space to reflect on what I enjoy about the F&B industry and where I want to focus my energy moving forward.

### The Pivot to Procurement

What I discovered during my time as a restaurant owner was that **procurement was one of the most impactful areas** I worked on. The decisions I made about:

- Which suppliers to work with
- How to negotiate pricing and terms
- When to buy in bulk vs. just-in-time
- How to balance quality vs. cost

...had a direct, measurable impact on the bottom line. Yet, I also noticed that **procurement is often under-optimized** in restaurants and hotels — treated as a transactional task rather than a strategic function.

### Where I Am Now

Today, I'm intentionally pivoting toward **procurement within the F&B sector** — specifically focusing on hotels, restaurants, and hospitality businesses in general.

My approach:

- **I come from operations**, so I understand the practical challenges end-users face
- **I build tools**, so I can automate repetitive tasks and create efficiency
- **I study systematically**, so I can understand the field deeply rather than superficially
- **I document openly**, so others can learn from my process and contribute their insights

### What I'm Studying & Building Right Now

- **Procurement frameworks** specific to F&B (perishable goods, seasonal pricing, supplier relationships)
- **Automation tools** using Python for purchase orders, invoice processing, and supplier communication
- **AI applications** for demand forecasting, vendor evaluation, and cost optimization
- **Data analysis** for spend management and supplier performance tracking

### Why This Portfolio Exists

I created this site for three reasons:

1. **Accountability** — Documenting my learning keeps me on track
2. **Sharing** — If my experiments and tools help even one person, it's worth it
3. **Connection** — I want to meet others interested in F&B procurement, automation, and operations innovation
