Sourcing Strategy · Domain — Category Strategy

Kraljic Matrix

Position purchase categories on supply risk versus profit impact to assign the right sourcing strategy to each. The foundational model for strategic sourcing — and the one that tells you which categories deserve a full total-cost-of-ownership analysis and which should simply be automated.

Purchase categories

CategorySupply riskProfit impactAnnual spend €

Supply risk (1–10): scarcity, few suppliers, lead-time, switching difficulty. Profit impact (1–10): share of spend, effect on cost or guest experience. Spend is optional — it sets the bubble size.

Strategic

High risk · high impact. Long-term partnerships, joint development, dual sourcing, risk mitigation.

Leverage

Low risk · high impact. Use buying power — competitive bidding, volume consolidation, price pressure.

Bottleneck

High risk · low impact. Secure supply — buffer stock, alternative sources, contingency plans.

Non-critical

Low risk · low impact. Simplify & automate — reduce transaction cost, e-catalogues, bundling.

The matrix

Pairs with the TCO tool: categories in the Strategic and Leverage quadrants — high profit impact — are where a full total-cost-of-ownership analysis pays off most. Non-critical items just need automating.
